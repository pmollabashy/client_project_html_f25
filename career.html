Career Exploration

Discover Your Career Path

Navigating your career journey is a cruicial part of your academic and professional life. An education in information opens doors to a diverse range of opportunities across many industries and sectors. Understanding the possibilities and aligning them with your unique skills, values, and interests is key to making informed decisions about your future.

This section is designed to provide you with the knowledge, resources, and support you need as you explore and evaluate your career options. Through self-assessment tools, industry insights, alumni stories, and guidance from career advisors, you can gain a comprehensive view of the roles and industries available to information professionals. Whether you are just beginning to explore your career interests or seeking to redefine your professional direction, we invite you to use these resources to thoughtfully plan and pursue a meaningful and rewarding career.

Career Exploration Steps

  1. Self-Reflection: Take the time to think about where your interests and skills align. What are you good at? What do you enjoy? What can you picture yourself doing?
  2. Research: Explore different industries and positions that seem interesting. Use resources like the UMSI Career Development website, to gain insights on different fields and what they entail.
  3. Utilize Resources: Seek guidance through career services and advisors to help you navigate the journey. Whether you're stuck, have concerns, or simply want some help, they're able to provide advice and resources to support your visions.
  4. Network: Reach out to professionals in fields you are interested in and set up meetings. Not only does this help build connections, but it allows you to gain insight on what certain positions are like. Attend networking events like career fairs and workshops to further expand your network.
  5. Internships: Gain real-world experience by pursuing internships in your field of interest. Practical experience will expose you to the work environment and hone your skills.
  6. Evaluate: After gaining knowledge and experience through research and internships, reflect on your options. What did you like or dislike? What are your short- and long-term goals, and how do these experiences align with them?
